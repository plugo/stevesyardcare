The Benefits of Aeration

Why is aeration recommended?

Aeration is the naturally occuring process of removing small plugs of thatch and soil from the lawn to improve soil aeration. Your lawn will be healthier and reduce its maintenance requirements.

When is the best time for aerating?

The best time for aeration is during the growing season, when the grass can heal and fill in any open areas after soil plugs are removed. Ideally, aerate the lawn with cool season grass in the early spring or fall and those with warm season grass in the late spring.

How Aeration Benefits Your Lawn

Lawn aeration helps alleviate compacted soil and thatch by creating holes in the ground, which allow air back down into the soil and create room for compacted soil to collapse back into a looser particle arrangement.

The result is more room for roots to expand and for helpful microorganisms to go about their business.
